# About Me
Hi! I am Pierre Joly. Since Mars 2026, I am a Research Engineer at <a href="https://www.cnrs.fr/en">CNRS</a> under the supervision of Hervé Turlier on "Information Emergence in Embryogenesis". In this position, I will work on developing multi-agent and physics-based simulations of embryogenesis, leveraging reinforcement learning and information-theoretic approaches to study how structure and function emerge in multicellular systems.

Previously, I was an intern at the <a href="https://sprintml.com">SprintML</a> lab (Apr–Sept 2025) under the supervision of Franziska Boenisch and Adam Dziedzic, where I worked on concept removal in text-to-image models. I also completed an internship at <a href="https://list.cea.fr/fr/">CEA-List</a> (May–Oct 2023) under the supervision of Mohamed Tamaazousti and Zakariya Chaouai, where I worked on uncertainty quantification for regression tasks applied to object detection models.

I graduated from <a href="https://www.ec-lyon.fr/en">École Centrale de Lyon</a> (Sept 2025) with a specialization in applied mathematics.
